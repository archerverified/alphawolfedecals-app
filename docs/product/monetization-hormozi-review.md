# Monetization Review — Hormozi Frameworks Applied

Date: 2026-06-10 · Companion to `prd-b2c-guided-design-flow.md` §5 · Frameworks: $100M Offers (Value Equation, guarantee types, value stack, MAGIC naming)

## The Value Equation, run on each side

`Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)`

**B2C customer (Casey):** Dream outcome = *seeing MY exact van wrapped, believably, before risking $3–30k*. Likelihood = high once the mockup uses her real template + her real photos + real film SKUs. Time delay = 15 minutes. Effort = a wizard. **Verdict: the B2C value equation is already strong — but it's strong as a LEAD MAGNET, not as the revenue engine.** Casey pays $20–60 in credits, once. Rico pays forever.

**B2B shop (Rico):** Dream outcome = *more wrap jobs walking in pre-sold, and 8 hours of design/paneling cycle collapsed to 1*. A wrap shop's average ticket is $2,500–5,000. One incremental job pays for a year of any plausible subscription. **Verdict: the shop side is where the money is, and the B2C flow is the machine that creates what shops value most — warm, spec-complete inbound leads.**

The strategic frame Hormozi would put on the whole product: **the free B2C design experience is the Grand Slam lead magnet for a B2B lead-gen + SaaS business.** Don't optimize B2C extraction; optimize B2C *volume* and monetize the shops who receive it.

## Review of current schemes (keep / fix)

| Scheme | Verdict | Hormozi note |
|---|---|---|
| Free initial design + free export | **KEEP — sacred** | This is the value-first flow that creates reciprocity and the viral PDF. Gating it would starve the lead engine. |
| Paid credits for post-initial edits | KEEP, but **rename and bundle by outcome** | "10 credits" is commodity pricing — it makes the customer do math. Sell named outcomes (below). |
| Vehicle slots (character-slot) | KEEP, minor SKU | Fine as-is. Won't move revenue; don't spend design energy here. |
| Digital-goods-only, no materials/quotes | KEEP | Right call — stays out of the shop's lane, which the affiliate play depends on. |
| B2B free during MVP | KEEP for now, **with a clock** | Free period is fine for validation, but announce the future price NOW ("founding shops: free until v2, then locked-in 50% off year one"). Free-forever expectations kill B2B launches. |

## Fixes to the B2C credit scheme (small money, but easy)

1. **Outcome-named bundles, not credit counts** (MAGIC naming):
   - *"Dial It In" pack* — enough iterations to take one design from concept to shop-ready (≈10 credits' worth).
   - *"Two More Looks" pack* — fresh concept directions after the freebies.
   - Show credits as the meter, sell the bundle as the outcome. Anchor the middle pack.
2. **Price anchor against the real alternative.** Shops charge $250–500 for a design consult; full wrap design services run $500+. The exhaustion sheet should say it: *"A shop design consult runs ~$300. Keep refining for $19."* That's a 10x+ value gap, stated, not implied.
3. **Conditional guarantee on paid packs:** *"If you don't land on a design you'd actually take to a shop, we put the credits back."* Cost of honoring ≈ inference pennies; trust unlock at checkout ≫ that. (Conditional type — tied to a stated outcome, abuse-resistant because the refund is credits, not cash.)
4. **Light urgency, honestly built:** monthly drip credits expire at month-end (use-it-or-lose-it is real scarcity, not fake countdown timers). Seasonal design-style drops ("Summer matte pack") create return visits without discounting.

## The real revenue ideas (ranked by money-in potential)

1. **Lead routing — charge shops for the warm inbound (v2 flagship).** Every export with the QR is a tracked, spec-complete lead. When a customer hits "submit to a shop" or a shop scans a pack's QR to claim the project: that's a qualified lead a shop would pay $25–100 for all day (their current lead sources are far worse). Start as per-lead, graduate to subscription tiers with included leads. **This monetizes the thing we already built** — the orders pipeline and QR attribution are live/specced.
2. **Shop SaaS tiers with a Grand Slam launch offer (v2, already roadmapped).** Shop Pro / Shop Studio per PRD v1.1 — but launched Hormozi-style: *"First 25 founding shops: if Wrap Studio doesn't save you 5+ hours on your first three projects, the year is free."* (Conditional guarantee + real scarcity of the founding cohort + a specific, measurable promise Rico can verify himself.) The paneling engine is the dream-outcome feature here — it's the 8-hours-to-1 collapse.
3. **Counter kiosk / embed mode (proven by AutoStyle.AI's model).** Shops rent the visualizer for their counter or embed it on their website ("Design your wrap" widget, branded to the shop, powered by Alpha Wolf). Flat monthly fee, ~$99–199/mo. Every kiosk session is also a platform signup. Two revenue streams from one feature: the shop pays for it AND it feeds the lead engine.
4. **Fleet pack — the high-ticket B2C-adjacent play.** Casey has 12 vans. A "Fleet Design Pack" (one approved design auto-rescaled across N saved vehicles + a consolidated multi-vehicle export) is worth real money to a business buyer — $299–499 one-time, no per-credit nickel-and-diming. Closest-to-money customer on the B2C side; do NOT price fleet buyers like consumers.
5. **Affiliate/referral fee from converted quotes (later, requires trust).** When a PDF converts at a shop and the shop is on-platform, a success fee or the lead-routing charge covers it. Off-platform shops: the PDF's QR is the acquisition hook, not a billing mechanism. Sequence this AFTER lead routing proves attribution works.

## What NOT to do (Hormozi anti-patterns present in most credit apps)

- Don't paywall the export — it's the referral engine wearing a deliverable costume.
- Don't price-discriminate the brief inputs (tint/PPF/chrome as paid add-ons was correctly rejected — charging to *describe* what you want is friction on the lead magnet).
- Don't launch B2B pricing without a guarantee — shops have been burned by software; the risk-reversal IS the offer.
- Don't add a subscription to B2C consumers in v1 — one-vehicle customers don't have recurring need; bundles match their episodic usage. (Fleet is the exception, and it's one-time-pack shaped anyway.)

## Sequencing (matches existing phasing)

- **Phase 2 (with AI + Stripe):** outcome-named bundles, anchor copy, conditional credits-back guarantee, expiring drip. Fleet pack if trivially close.
- **v2 B2B launch:** founding-shop Grand Slam offer + lead routing per-lead billing + announce-the-clock on free MVP shops.
- **v2.x:** kiosk/embed mode, affiliate mechanics on proven QR attribution.

**One-line summary:** B2C credits keep the lights on; the export pack builds the shop waiting list; lead routing + shop SaaS with a founding-cohort guarantee is where the real money comes in.
